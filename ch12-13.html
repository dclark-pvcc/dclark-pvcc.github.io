Chapter 12: Models of Computation
What is the purpose of a model?
  • Studying many physical and social phenomena
List four phenomena of a model:
  1. Captures the essence—the important properties—of the real thing.
  2. Probably differs in scale from the real thing.
  3. Omits some of the details of the real thing.
  4. Lacks the full functionality of the real thing.
List four things a computing agent should be able to do:
  1. Accept input
  2. Store information in memory and retrieve it from memory
  3. Take actions according to algorithm instructions
  4. Produce output
List four characteristics of an algorithm:
  1. Be a well-ordered collection
  2. Consist of unambiguous and effectively computable operations
  3. Halt in a finite amount of time
  4. Produce a result
What is the "halting problem" with regards to an uncomputable problem?
  • There is no universal program that can correctly answer the halting problem for all possible programs and inputs, where yes(it will halt) and no(it will run forever).
Chapter 13: Simulation & Modeling
Define these terms:
  1. Scientific method: Observing the behavior of a system and formulating a hypothesis that tries to understand and explain that behavior.
  2. Computational model: A model constructed using algorithmic procedures implemented as computer programs.
  3. Discrete Event Simulation: A computational modeling technique that simulates the behavior of a system only at discrete points in time.
  4. List and describe seven reasons to construct a simulation model.
    • Reduces risk and avoids costly errors
    • Saves time and money on experiments
    • See how a system's components interact over time.
    • Gain insight on potential growth for the program.
    • Handles randomness in systems.
    • Slow or speed up a process.
    • Use visualization for better communication.
Illustris: "The Mother of All Computations"
  1. What is Illustris?: The most detailed and realistic computer simulation ever built to study the formation of the universe.
  2. What fields of science were involved in the Illustris computational model?: Physics, astronomy, hydrodynamics, chemistry, and mathematics.
  3. What does Illustris simulate?: A cube of simulated space that is 350 light years on each side and that contains tens of thousands of galaxies.
  4. How long did the project take?: 5 years of development.
  5. How many years of gallactic evolution are simulated in Illustris?: 14 billion years of galactic evolution.
  6. Illustris was created using two supercomputers. What is a supercomputer?
    • A supercomputer is capable of processing massive amounts of data and solving complex problems much faster than a standard computer. It acts on a much higher level of performance.
